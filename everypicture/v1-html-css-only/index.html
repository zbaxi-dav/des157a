Ingredients

  • ¾ cup short-grain rice
  • 1½–2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ¼ tsp turmeric (or saffron if you want the authentic touch)
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Topping Suggestions

  • Red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • Cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • Artichoke hearts
  • Cooked chickpeas
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions

  1. Prep the Pan
    • Heat olive oil in a skillet (ideally wide and shallow) over medium heat.
    • Add onion and garlic, cook 3–4 minutes until softened.
  2. Build Flavor
    • Stir in smoked paprika and turmeric (or saffron). Toast the spices for 30 seconds.
    • Add rice and stir to coat grains with oil and spices. Cook 1–2 minutes until rice edges look translucent.
  3. Add Liquids & Veggies
    • Pour in warm broth (start with 1½ cups) and stir once to spread rice evenly, then do not stir again (helps form the crust).
    • Add bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, artichokes, and chickpeas, arranging them decoratively if you like.
    • Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook the Paella
    • Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cook uncovered 20–25 minutes, until rice is tender and most liquid is absorbed.
    • If rice isn’t done but pan is dry, splash in more warm broth.
  5. Create Socarrat (Optional but Authentic)
    • Once rice is cooked, increase heat to medium-high for 1–2 minutes to toast the bottom slightly (it should smell toasty, not burnt).
  6. Finish & Serve
    • Remove from heat, cover with a clean towel, and let rest 5 minutes.
    • Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges.
